# Accessible Housing — Complete Taxonomies

All taxonomy values below are the **canonical terms** for this system. Use these exact strings when populating records. Each value includes a plain-language definition for use in user-facing output.

## Housing Control Model

| Value | Plain-Language Definition |
|---|---|
| Consumer-controlled | The home and services are not connected. You can change services while staying in the home. |
| Hybrid | Some control is yours and some belongs to the provider. |
| Provider-controlled | Services are connected to the home. If you leave the home, you lose services. |

## Availability Status

| Value | Plain-Language Definition |
|---|---|
| Planning stages | The housing is not yet available. It is still being planned. |
| Available | The housing is currently available. |
| Waitlist | The housing is full. You can sign up to be notified when something opens. |

## Support Levels

| Value | Plain-Language Definition |
|---|---|
| No Support Needs | I do not need any support staff at home. |
| Drop-In Support | I only need someone to check in on me every few days. |
| Low Support | I need someone to help with a few small tasks each day then leave. |
| Moderate Support | I need someone to check in several times per day. I can be okay on my own for several hours. |
| 24/7 Support | I need someone nearby at all times (and I can share staff). |
| One-on-One Support | I need someone's attention at all times. |
| Daily Medical Support | I need daily support with medical equipment and medical check-ins. |
| Memory Care | I often forget things and need someone to guide me throughout the day. |
| High Behavioral Support | I need support with not hurting myself, others, or items around me. |

## Program Model

| Value | Plain-Language Definition |
|---|---|
| Agency-based program | An outside group takes care of my staffing needs. |
| Host home | I live with others in their home. |
| Intermediate care facility | I live in a healthcare building with many staff and nurses who treat me. |
| Natural supports | Neighbors, friends, or family members support me at home. |
| Remote support | I use technology to get support from someone who is not nearby. |
| Residential transition program | I live here temporarily while I gain skills in living on my own. |
| Self-directed support | I am responsible for finding and hiring support staff. |
| Shared living | I share my living space with someone who provides support in the home. |

## Funding

| Value | Plain-Language Definition |
|---|---|
| Intermediate care facility funding | The state government pays for treatment at a healthcare building. |
| Medicaid HCBS waiver | Support staff is paid for by a state program (ECF CHOICES or CHOICES in Tennessee). |
| Medicare | For seniors whose support staff is paid for by the federal government. |
| Private Pay | You or someone you know will pay. |
| Section 8 / Housing Choice Voucher | Allows you or your family member to live where they want to live. |

| Unknown | Funding information is not yet known. |

## Development Type

| Value | Plain-Language Definition |
|---|---|
| Assisted living | A place with 25+ people who receive support in their room or apartment. |
| Scattered site | A home in a general neighborhood, not in a community designed for any one group. **(Integrated setting)** |
| Group home | A home where 2–6 people share common areas and support staff and have their own rooms. |
| Group living | A home with 3–4 private bedrooms and shared common spaces. |
| Planned or intentional community | A housing community with multiple homes or apartments designed for the people who live there. **(Disability-specific setting)** |
| Mixed-use development | Housing that also has other things on the land nearby, such as shops. |
| Cohousing community | A housing community designed and managed by the people who live there. |

|---|---|

## Detailed Accessibility Checklists

Use these when verifying or documenting a property beyond the high-level amenity tags.

### Mobility Accessibility

- [ ] Accessible route from parking to unit (no steps or ramp available)
- [ ] Accessible parking close to unit entrance
- [ ] Accessible elevators (if multi-story)
- [ ] First floor units available
- [ ] Doorways 32"+ clear width
- [ ] Thresholds 1/2" or less at doors/flooring transitions
- [ ] Lever-style door handles and faucets
- [ ] Accessible light switches (lowered height)
- [ ] Accessible flooring for wheelchairs, walkers, scooters, crutches
- [ ] Low counters with 27" knee space underneath
- [ ] Front controls on stove/cooktop
- [ ] Counter-height microwave
- [ ] Accessible cabinet hardware
- [ ] T-turn space or 60" turning circle in bathroom
- [ ] Toilet grab bars or reinforcements
- [ ] Bath/shower grab bars or reinforcements
- [ ] Accessible height toilet (17–19")
- [ ] Roll-in shower option
- [ ] Walk-in shower option
- [ ] Front-control laundry machines
- [ ] Accessible route to laundry facilities

### Sensory Accessibility

- [ ] Visual alarms / strobe alerts
- [ ] Audible alerts and announcements
- [ ] Visual doorbells / notifications
- [ ] Braille or tactile signage
- [ ] High contrast design elements
- [ ] Good lighting throughout
- [ ] Video relay or communication devices available

### Cognitive Accessibility

- [ ] Clear wayfinding and signage
- [ ] Sensory-friendly design (low stimulation options)
- [ ] Quiet spaces available
- [ ] Predictable, consistent layouts
- [ ] Visual schedules / supports available

## Disability Type Reference

This system recognizes the full spectrum of disabilities. When a user searches by condition, map it to the appropriate category AND store the specific condition.

**Categories:** Physical Disabilities (Mobility Impairments, Other Physical) · Sensory Disabilities (Visual, Hearing, Other Sensory) · Intellectual and Developmental Disabilities (Intellectual, Developmental, Learning) · Neurological Disabilities (Brain Injuries, Seizure Disorders, Neurodegenerative, Other) · Mental Health Disabilities (Mood, Anxiety, Psychotic, Personality, Eating, Other) · Chronic Health Conditions (Respiratory, Cardiovascular, Autoimmune/Inflammatory, Metabolic/Endocrine, Kidney/Liver, Blood, Cancer, Immune System, Gastrointestinal, Other) · Speech and Communication Disabilities · Multiple/Complex Disabilities

**Functional Categories:** Mobility Support Needs · Communication Support Needs · Medical Support Needs
