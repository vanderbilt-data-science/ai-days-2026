# Inside Agentic AI: Behind the Scenes

**Thursday, March 5, 2026**  
**9:00 AM – 10:00 AM** (60 minutes)

**Presenter:** Charreau Bell, Ph.D.

## Session Description

Drawn from Course 1: Foundations of Generative AI and Course 4: Agentic Solution Development and Deployment. AI tools have evolved from simple chatbots into sophisticated systems that can plan, use tools, and iterate on their own work. But how? This session pulls back the curtain on agentic AI — the architectures and design patterns that enable AI systems to take meaningful action. We'll walk through five workflow patterns for orchestrating AI in controlled, predictable ways, then dive into how autonomous AI agents actually work: the agent loop, context management, tool execution, and the infrastructure that holds it all together. The session includes a hands-on demo building AI workflows with visual, no-code platforms. No technical background required.

## About the Instructor

### Charreau Bell, Ph.D.

**Senior Data Scientist, Vanderbilt Data Science Institute; Assistant Professor of the Practice of Computer Science; Faculty Director, Undergraduate Data Science Minor**

Charreau Bell leads data science projects across a spectrum of disciplines using artificial intelligence (AI), machine learning,  and deep learning at Vanderbilt's Data Science Institute. She has taught several undergraduate and graduate courses on generative AI at Vanderbilt, including a course on constructing and deploying AI systems on cloud-based architecture including AWS. She serves as faculty director of the undergraduate data science minor, co-authored SACSCOC guidance on AI for universities, and has been featured as a faculty expert in a virtual panel hosted by the Southeastern Conference (SEC) on generative artificial intelligence. A Triple Dore, she earned her bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees in electrical engineering at Vanderbilt.
