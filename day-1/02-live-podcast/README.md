## About the Instructor

### Matthew Johnson-Roberson, Ph.D.

**Dean, College of Connected Computing; Professor of Computer Science and Electrical and Computer Engineering**

Matthew Johnson-Roberson is the inaugural Dean of Vanderbilt's College of Connected Computing, the university's first new college in over 40 years. A distinguished scholar with over 100 peer-reviewed publications, his research spans underwater robotics, computer vision, and autonomous vehicles. He previously directed Carnegie Mellon University's Robotics Institute and held faculty positions at the University of Michigan, where he co-directed the UM Ford Center for Autonomous Vehicles. He co-founded Refraction AI, developing autonomous delivery robots for sustainable urban last-mile delivery. He earned his Ph.D. in robotics from the University of Sydney. His leadership of the College of Connected Computing emphasizes interdisciplinary collaboration and empowering students to harness computational thinking across disciplines.
