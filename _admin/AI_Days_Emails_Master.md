**Subject:** AI Days Workshops March 4-5

```
Good morning, Prof. Graham,

I wanted to reach out about upcoming workshops on AI we've designed for Vanderbilt faculty. The College of Connected Computing is hosting AI Days on March 4–5 at the Vanderbilt Hilton — a two-day program designed to help researchers evaluate and adopt AI agents in their own research as a new research capability. It's free and open to Vanderbilt faculty, postdocs, staff, and graduate students space permitting.

Given the complexity of modern genomic and proteomic datasets, I thought you'd find particular value in our hands-on sessions on AI agents for data processing and visualization — tools that can help your researchers extract insights from large-scale biological datasets without requiring coding expertise.

Day 1 covers the practical foundations: how current models and agents actually work, IP and privacy considerations for research protocols, and the cognitive pitfalls of human-AI collaboration. Day 2 is hands-on. Faculty will build domain-specific AI research assistants and learn to use AI coding agents to create functional tools — data processing scripts, visualization dashboards, analysis pipelines — through specification writing rather than programming. No prior coding experience is needed. The program wraps with a working session where participants build something they can take back to their lab.

The program is pitched at researchers, not technologists. The goal is to move faculty beyond chat and into the far more advanced capabilities of agentic AI — and to give them a calibrated understanding of what these tools can and can't do, a disciplined framework for deciding when AI augmentation is appropriate, and practical experience building tools tailored to their domain.

Faculty can register at https://www.vanderbilt.edu/datascience/ai-days-2026/

I'm happy to answer any questions about the program — feel free to call (217-377-2867) or email me (jesse.spencer-smith@vanderbilt.edu) at any time.

Warm regards,

Jesse
```
