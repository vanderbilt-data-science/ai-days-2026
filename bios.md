# AI Days Instructor Bios


## Jesse Spencer-Smith, Ph.D.

Interim Director and Chief Data Scientist, Vanderbilt Data Science Institute; Associate Dean for Partnerships and Innovation, College of Connected Computing; Professor of the Practice of Computer Science

Jesse Spencer-Smith leads Vanderbilt's Data Science Institute and a team of data scientists collaborating with researchers and industry partners across the university. He holds a Ph.D. in Cognitive Psychology and Cognitive Science from Indiana University and a B.S. in Computer Science from the University of Florida. Before joining Vanderbilt, he served as Director of Data Science at HCA Healthcare, where he built the company's first data science team and facilitated data science implementation across the enterprise. He previously held an appointment as Assistant Professor of Psychology in Quantitative Methods at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign and was a Beckman Fellow at the Beckman Institute for Advanced Science and Technology. His interdisciplinary background in psychology, cognitive science, and computer science informs his approach to advancing data-driven insights and AI applications. He was recently appointed to the Tennessee AI Advisory Council by Governor Bill Lee and serves as faculty head of Sutherland House.


## Matthew Johnson-Roberson, Ph.D.

Dean, College of Connected Computing; Professor of Computer Science and Electrical and Computer Engineering

Matthew Johnson-Roberson is the inaugural Dean of Vanderbilt's College of Connected Computing, the university's first new college in over 40 years. A distinguished scholar with over 100 peer-reviewed publications, his research spans underwater robotics, computer vision, and autonomous vehicles. He previously directed Carnegie Mellon University's Robotics Institute and held faculty positions at the University of Michigan, where he co-directed the UM Ford Center for Autonomous Vehicles. He co-founded Refraction AI, developing autonomous delivery robots for sustainable urban last-mile delivery. He earned his Ph.D. in robotics from the University of Sydney. His leadership of the College of Connected Computing emphasizes interdisciplinary collaboration and empowering students to harness computational thinking across disciplines.


## Charreau Bell, Ph.D.

Senior Data Scientist, Vanderbilt Data Science Institute; Assistant Professor of the Practice of Computer Science; Faculty Director, Undergraduate Data Science Minor

Charreau Bell leads data science projects across a spectrum of disciplines using artificial intelligence (AI), machine learning, and deep learning at Vanderbilt's Data Science Institute. She has taught several undergraduate and graduate courses on generative AI at Vanderbilt, including a course on constructing and deploying AI systems on cloud-based architecture including AWS. She serves as faculty director of the undergraduate data science minor, co-authored SACSCOC guidance on AI for universities, and has been featured as a faculty expert on legal podcasts and virtual panels hosted by the Southeastern Conference (SEC) on generative AI. A Triple Dore, she earned her bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees in electrical engineering at Vanderbilt.


## Umang Chaudhry

Senior Data Scientist, Vanderbilt Data Science Institute

Umang Chaudhry provides consultation on data science initiatives spanning both academic and industry sectors at Vanderbilt's Data Science Institute. His expertise encompasses machine learning, deep learning, and cloud computing. He leads cross-disciplinary research teams and develops AI solutions focused on assistive technologies, including AI-powered tools for neurodivergent individuals. He has also worked on addressing challenges faced by low-resource languages, developing an AI assistant for Hindi and Sanskrit language instruction. He came to Vanderbilt in 2015 as an undergraduate, earning B.A. degrees in Earth and Environmental Sciences (Honors) and Communication of Science and Technology, followed by an M.S. in Data Science from Vanderbilt.


## Abbie Petulante, Ph.D.

Data Scientist, Vanderbilt Data Science Institute

Abbie Petulante completed her Ph.D. in Astronomy at Vanderbilt, where she applied machine learning and deep learning techniques to model the development of large-scale dark matter structures in simulations of the evolution of the universe. As a member of the DSI data science team, she works on projects applying deep learning models to scientific problems through research collaborations across the university. Her postdoctoral work was supported by Vanderbilt's Deep Learning Initiative, which advances the adoption of AI techniques across academic disciplines.


## Myranda Shirk

Senior Data Scientist, Vanderbilt Data Science Institute

Myranda Uselton Shirk is a Vanderbilt Data Science Institute alum who returned to DSI as a Senior Data Scientist. She holds an M.S. in Data Science from Vanderbilt and has dedicated her career to advancing neurodiverse workforces. Before rejoining the DSI, she worked for over two years as a Data Scientist at NOAA's National Centers for Environmental Information. Her experience also includes software development at James E. Walker Library and XR development at NASA Glenn Research Center, where she developed AR/VR applications. At Vanderbilt, she has led work on AI-powered tools for neurodivergent individuals and organized sessions on AI-assisted programming, including participation in OceanHackWeek.


## Jesse Blocher, Ph.D.

Associate Professor of the Practice of Data Science and Finance; Director of Graduate Studies, M.S. in Data Science

Jesse Blocher has been on the faculty at Vanderbilt's Owen Graduate School of Management since 2012. In 2018 he co-founded the Data Science Institute and began developing coursework for the Master's in Data Science program, becoming Director of Graduate Studies for the M.S. program in 2021. He brings nine years of professional experience as a consultant, educator, and software developer prior to earning his Ph.D. in Finance from UNC Chapel Hill. He also holds an M.S. in Chemical Engineering Practice from MIT and a B.S. in Chemical Engineering from Virginia Tech. His research bridges finance and data science, with focus areas including short sales and securities lending, asset management, network effects, and high-frequency trading. He has received the Financial Research Association's Michael J. Barclay Award and the Shmuel Kandel Award for exceptional doctoral research in Financial Economics.


## Miguel Nunez

Marketing Manager, Vanderbilt Data Science Institute

Miguel Nunez serves as Marketing Manager for Vanderbilt's Data Science Institute, where he has been involved in data science projects including developing machine learning solutions and AI-driven assistant tools.


## Mark Williams

VAILL Co-Director; Professor of the Practice of Law, Vanderbilt Law School

Mark Williams oversees the collections services department for Vanderbilt's Alyne Queener Massey Law Library and teaches courses in Advanced Legal Research on Business and Securities and Legal Practice Technology. He co-directs the Vanderbilt AI Law Lab (VAILL) and has created coursework covering ethics and privacy, prompt engineering strategies for legal use cases, and leveraging generative AI in legal services. He leads the "Generative AI for Legal Services Primer" available on Coursera and has organized faculty bootcamps introducing Large Language Models and generative AI technology. He has also co-organized Vanderbilt Law School's inaugural AI Governance Symposium.
